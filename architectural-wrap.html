FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Explore our comprehensive FAQ to find answers to all your questions about working with architectural films and Brand Works.

How long does architectural wrap last?

Not all vinyl is created equal. Architectural film boasts superior quality and durability, lasting 7 - 10 years (often longer) with proper care. It even features a scratch - resistant layer to handle everyday wear and tear. High - traffic areas may experience more wear, so keep that in mind when choosing a surface.

Are the films you use fire rated?

Absolutely, all our architectural vinyl meets fire safety standards. We use three trusted brands: 3M, Cove rstyl, and LX. • 3M DI - NOC films are specifically certified to meet CE and EN 13501 fire safety standards. • Coverstyl films have also undergone rigorous fire resistance testing and meet European fire rating standards. • LX films are recognized for their safety, passing both domestic and global fire safety certifications. Need a specific fire certification for your project? No problem! We'll be happy to provide the relevant documentation based on the film you choose. Just get in touch with our team and we'll ensu re you have the information you need.

How long does vinyl wrapping take?

We understand minimizing downtime is crucial for your business. Architectural vinyl wraps offer a significant advantage in turnaround time compared to traditional renovations. The sp eed depends on the project's size and complexity, but for smaller jobs, we can often complete them within a single day.

For example, our team has successfully wrapped 24 entire hotel rooms in a day, including desks, headboards, cabinets, and doors! This de monstrates our expertise and ability to handle projects efficiently.

In most cases, we can be in and out of your business within a day, minimizing disruption and getting you back to normal operations quickly. For a more accurate estimate on your specific p roject, feel free to contact us with details about the scope of work.

What if my surface has existing damage? Can it still be wrapped?

We can often address minor surface imperfections like scratches, dents, and small holes as part of our vinyl wrap prepara tion process. Our skilled technicians can fill and repair these areas, ensuring a flawless finish once the wrap is applied. In most cases, you'd never know there was any damage beforehand!

However, it's important to be realistic about the extent of damage. If the surface is severely compromised, like being completely broken in half, wrapping might not be the most suitable solution. In those cases, replacing the surface entirely might be the best course of action.

The best way to determine if wrapping is rig ht for your project is to schedule a consultation. Our team can assess the specific damage and advise you on the most effective and long - lasting solution.

Will the vinyl peel, fade or lift after a few years?

Architectural vinyl is renowned for its exceptional durability. When properly installed, you can expect minimal risk of peeling or fading over time. To ensure a flawless finish, our team takes extra precautions. We'll prime any areas where even the slightest peeling might occur, giving you compl ete peace of mind about the longevity of your wrap.

Can I install the vinyl myself?

While architectural vinyl offers a fantastic aesthetic upgrade, professional installation is highly recommended to achieve the best possible results. These films require s pecialized techniques and experience to ensure a flawless, long - lasting finish.

Here's why opting for professional installation is a wise choice:

• Expert Application: Our skilled installers have the experience and knowledge to handle even the most intricate surfaces, guaranteeing a smooth, bubble - free application. • Seamless Finish: Professionals know how to properly prepare surfaces and use the right tools to achieve a flawless, high - quality look. • Peace of Mind: By letting our experts handle the installation, you can be confident that your vinyl wrap will look its best and last for years to come.
Of course, the final decision is yours. If you're an experienced installer comfortable working with specialized vinyl, you can certainly tackle the project yourself. However, for most customers, professional installation offers significant advantages and ensures a truly exceptional outcome.
